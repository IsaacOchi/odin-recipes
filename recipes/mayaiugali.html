Mayai Ugali Recipe

Mayai Ugali is a traditional dish from the coastal region of Kenya. It is a delicious combination of maize flour and eggs, often served with vegetables or meat.

This recipe is easy to follow and can be made in under an hour. It is a great option for breakfast, lunch, or dinner.

Mayai Ugali is not only tasty but also nutritious, making it a perfect meal for the whole family. Enjoy this delightful dish with your favorite sides!

Cooking Time

Preparation Time: 20 minutes

Cooking Time: 30 minutes

Total Time: 50 minutes

Servings

This recipe serves 2 people.

Difficulty Level

Easy

Ingredients

  • 2 cups maize flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 bunch of spinach
  • 1 table spoon of salt
  • Cooking oil
  • 1 large bulb onion
  • 2 tomatoes
  • 1 bunch of corriander
  • 1 cube chicken boulion

Preparation

  1. Chop spinnach, onions, tomatoes and corriander.
  2. In a pan over medium heat, 5 tablespoons of oil, 3/4 of the chopped onions. Cook until golden brown.
  3. Add all the chopped tomatoes. Cook until they become soft.
  4. Add chicken boulion in a quater cup of water. Pour it ovet the tomatoes and let it cook for 5 mins
  5. Add two beaten eggs. Stir continously to form chunks. Add salt to taste.
  6. Add the chopped corriander for garnishing. Set aside
  7. In a pan over medium heat. Add 3 tablespoons of oil and the remaining onions.
  8. Cook until golden brown.
  9. Add the chopped spinnach and cook for 5 min stirring continously. Add salt to taste. Set aside
  10. In a pot over medium heat, add 1 and 1/2 cups of water.
  11. Once the water boils, add the flour in portions, kneading with a wooden spoon untill a medium-hard dough is formed.
  12. At this point, knead until no dry patches of flour are visible. Shape the dough in to a circular shape.
  13. Cover the pot and let it cook for 5 mins. Its ready when it gives off a sweat baked smell. Set aside
  14. Serve and enjoy
